The Calvinvill National Basketball League (CNBL) is a professional basketball league in Calvinvill and Khouza consisting of ten teams split into two divisions, the A division and Z division respectively. The type of basketball played is unique to the Kouz Island chain as each team has three players on the court and games being confined to a point total of 30. Many of the players are homegrown, often being drafted from various universities after playing Calvinvill College Basketball.

The island now known as Calvinvill was first inhabited by Berber or Amazigh peoples. The Berbers, indigenous to North Africa, particularly modern-day Morocco, Algeria, and Libya, originally spread Westward from the Nile. Around 703 CE (5037 KC), as the Umayyad Caliphate and Islam moved Westward, small groups of Berbers fled into the Mediterranean Sea and discovered the Kouz Islands chain.


Population centers were on the two main islands, Calvinvill, then called Tasrifa, and Khouza. The settlements, initially composed of hundreds of people, were a refuge from Umayyad rule, other Berbers, and conversion to Islam. The Berbers of Kouz maintained their traditional cultural and religious practices partially a result of the relative geographic isolation of the islands.

In the following centuries, these intial settlements expanded and a clear distinction developed between the Berbers of the Tasrifan Hinterlands, the Tasrifan Coast, and the surrounding Islands. Secluded by the surrounded mountain ranges, the Berbers of the Hinterlands were the most sedentary. Since the Hinterland Berbers lived in the most fertile land, they raised producive agricultural fields and livestock such as sheep and goats. The clans of the Hinterland grew more intertwined and centralized. Whereas, the Berbers of the Tasrifan Coast and Kouz Islands, did not have access to such fertile lands. The livelihood of these clans were dependent on fishing and trade with the low Hinterlands, creating an inherent power imbalance between the Hinterlands, Coastal, and Island clans.
