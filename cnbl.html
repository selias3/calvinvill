The Calvinvill National Basketball League (CNBL) is a professional basketball league in Calvinvill and Khouza consisting of ten teams split into two divisions, the A Division and Z Division respectively. The type of basketball played is unique to the Kouz Island chain, as each team has three players on the court and games are confined to a point total of 30. Many of the players are homegrown, often being drafted from various universities after playing Calvinvill College Basketball.

The island now known as Calvinvill was first inhabited by Berber or Amazigh peoples. The Berbers, indigenous to North Africa, particularly modern-day Morocco, Algeria, and Libya, originally spread westward from the Nile. Around 703 CE (5037 KC), as the Umayyad Caliphate and Islam moved westward, small groups of Berbers fled into the Mediterranean Sea and discovered the Kouz Islands chain.

Population centers were on the two main islands, Calvinvill, then called Tasrifa, and Khouza. The settlements, initially composed of hundreds of people, were a refuge from Umayyad rule, other Berbers, and conversion to Islam. The Berbers of Kouz maintained their traditional cultural and religious practices partially as a result of the relative geographic isolation of the islands.

In the following centuries, these initial settlements expanded and a clear distinction developed between the Berbers of the Tasrifan Hinterlands, the Tasrifan Coast, and the surrounding islands. Secluded by the surrounding mountain ranges, the Berbers of the Hinterlands were the most sedentary. Since they lived in the most fertile land, they developed productive agricultural fields and raised livestock such as sheep and goats.

The clans of the Hinterlands grew more intertwined and centralized. In contrast, the Berbers of the Tasrifan Coast and Kouz Islands did not have access to such fertile lands. Their livelihoods depended on fishing and trade with the Hinterlands, creating an inherent power imbalance between the Hinterland, Coastal, and Island clans.
